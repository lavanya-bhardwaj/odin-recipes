Description

This chocolate magic cake separates into 3 magic layers while baking: a light cakey top, jello-like center, and a fudgy bottom, and you will see all 3 layers in a cut slice. It has a light chocolate flavor, like milk chocolate, with an espresso bite.

Ingredients

  • 4 large egg whites
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup butter, melted
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon instant espresso powder (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder or Dutch-process cocoa powder
  • 2 cups milk, heated to lukewarm
  • 1 tablespoon powdered sugar

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C). Grease an 8x8-inch baking dish or pan.
  2. Beat egg whites with an electric mixer on medium speed in a medium glass or metal bowl until stiff peaks form. Set aside.
  3. Beat white sugar and egg yolks with an electric mixer on medium speed in a large bowl until fluffy and lightened in color, about 2 minutes.
  4. Gradually beat in melted butter, vanilla, instant espresso powder (if using), and salt until smooth and thickened, about 2 minutes.
  5. Mix in flour and cocoa powder just until combined. Gradually beat in milk until smooth.
  6. Gently fold egg whites into the batter using a spatula until partially combined. Small lumps and streaks of egg white should still be visible; this will help create the cake's distinct layers. Transfer batter to prepared baking dish.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven until top is golden and an instant-read thermometer inserted in the center registers 195 to 200 degrees F (91 to 93 degrees C), 45 to 50 minutes. The center should still jiggle slightly when gently shaken.
  8. Cool to room temperature on a wire rack, 1 1/2 to 2 hours. Cover and refrigerate until cold, 2 to 4 hours. Chilling also helps the layers fully set.
  9. Dust with powdered sugar before serving.
